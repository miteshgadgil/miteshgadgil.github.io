DATA ANALYSIS & STATISTICAL INFERENCE CASE STUDIES

PROJECTS UNDERTAKEN AS PART OF A GRADUATE COURSE AT UCSD

INTRODUCTION

I chose to do a graduate course offered by the Mathematics & Statistics Department at UCSD: 'Exploratory Data analysis & Statistical Inference'. My motivation for undertaking this course as an elective was that the course would give me a good foundation in statistical concepts used in data analysis and help me gain experience using R programming. The course included 4 case-studies which explored the variety in the kinds of datasets and analysis tools used. Each group of 2-3 students submitted a R-code file and a report of the analysis and findings.
I earned an A+ grade in the course. In the sections below I have included a brief background of the problem posed by each case study and a link to the code and writeup my team submitted for the same.


  1. Is the birth weight of an infant affected by whether the mother smokes during pregnancy?
  2. Recommendations for the design of a Computer Lab based on survey data of a class of students
  3. Establish which sites in the DNA sequence maybe sites of replication for the Cytomegalovirus (CMV)
  4. Estimate density of the accumulated snow based on readings of the gamma transmission snow gauge

Case Study 1

The objective of this case study is to understand the various options available for 'Exploratory data analysis' of a dataset to get an idea of whether or not a particular feature affects the quantity of interest. In this particular dataset, we want to understand if the birthweight of an infant is affected by maternal smoking during pregnancy.
We have conducted the analysis using scatterplots, histograms, stacked barplots, q-q plots and box plots for multi-dimensional data (using color/shape attributes). The main focus of the exploration was to ascertain that there were no confounding variables especially income, education, gestation period, etc. causing low birthweight.
The important takewaway was that correlation does't imply causation.

Case Study 2

Every year, approximately 1000 students enrol in the introductory statistics course at UC Berkeley. To aid the instruction of these students, a commitee of faculty and students were to design a series of computer labs that would provide an interactive learning environment. A survey of undergraduate students enrolled in a lower division statistics course was conducted to determine the extent to which students play video games and which aspects of video games do they find most and least fun. The aim of this case study is to study the data of the survey and make recommendations about the design of computer labs that would be most appealing to students.

Case Study 3

The Human Cytomegalovirus(CMV) is a potentially life threatening disease. In order to combat the virus, virologists suggest that the origin of replication of the virus should be identified and their reproduction be stopped. Research has shown that clusters of palindromes in the DNA sequence have a high probability of being sites of replication. Given that the DNA is a long sequence of 4 base amino-acids, many palindrome sequences might occur by random chance. The objective of the case study is to model the occurence of palindromes in the DNA sequence and pick sites in the DNA with a statistically different number of palindrome clusters compared to what we would expect by random chance. Picking out such potential sites can help virologists conduct tests starting at these locations before moving on to other sites in the DNA.

Case Study 4

In order to monitor the water supply from yearly snowfall, the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) uses a gamma transmission snow gauge. These gauges are placed in areas of interest, including the Sierra Nevada Mountains, which supplies water to Northern California. The gauge determines the depth of the snowfall and provides a measurement termed as 'gain' based on the gamma rays incident on the snow. In order to provide an estimate of the volume of water that will be available for the upcoming year, we require a good estimate of the density of the snow. The objective of the case study is to study a dataset of measurements of gain for different known densities, in order to deduce a relationship to convert the observed 'gain' into a precise and accurate estimate of density. We have used a linear regression model for this purpose and studied all the inherent assumptions of a linear model.
