ABOUT THE PROJECT

I chose to participate in the BNP Paribas Cardif Claims Management Kaggle Contest as the capstone project for the EDA & Statistical Inference graduate course at UCSD. This was my first time participating in a kaggle contest and it was a highly enjoyable and enriching experience. My focus throughout the project was to implement the whole data analysis workflow in R- starting with cleaning and upto validation of the model.

The challenge was a binary classification problem where we had to predict the probability of a particular claim being suitable for accelerated approval based on a number of anonymized features. The fact that there were a lot of features of different cardinality, scale and all of them anonymized made this challenge particularly difficult and gave me an opportunity to learn about working with data in a domain agnostic manner and purely on statistics. I pre-processed the data into a suitable form for applying ML models on it. I experimented with the generalized linear model (GLM), Random Forests and Xgboost packages in R. Xgboost which is the most popular ML algorithm in Kaggle contests in recent times, gave me the best results and fetched me a rank in the top half of the field of over 2000 participants.
